Josh sent me to Stackoverflow back in September of 2008. When I took a look I thought it looked interesting but I couldn't see actually trying to keep up on what looked to be several updates per minute. Plus I did not have any immediate questions I needed to have answered so I moved on, and never looked back. Until yesterday. Now I am hooked. Josh has asked me several times over the last few years if I was involved in any online software communities. Message boards, blogs or IRC channels really never seemed all that enjoyable to me, even though I do enjoy talking about code and working with other people on problems. Stackoverflow looks to be the place to get involved and discuss things without having to get overly bogged down in long forum posts or keeping an IRC window open all day long. It is not just about asking questions. It actually seems to be at least as much about answering questions. Which is far more interesting for me about 95% of the time! What surprises me is that I have not stumbled onto this site during all of my software searched on Google. This site is far more fun and useful that Experts Exchange I have grown to hate. Hopefully it starts popping up in more Google searches as time goes on...
