Yeah. Gotta love copy and paste errors leading to several hours of lost development time.

I had initially copied an xmlns namespace URI from actual AWS S3 XML output so I assumed it was pure and clean. It turns out that it was not nearly as clean as I had hoped. Somehow the URI had lost a / along the way.

https://gist.github.com/288208

Since PHP XPath is stupid when it comes to documents with a default xmlns, having an xmlns registered was required and since it was not pasted correctly (with no warnings stating as much) none of my XPath queries would give me any results.

Having never actually had PHP XPath function correctly, I really had no idea what could be wrong and focused mainly on XPath dialect and various combinations of prefixes and queries.

Thinking it could have been something as simple as a typo in the namespace UI I did actually do a few fresh copy and pastes of the URI several times.

At this point I am not even sure how the URI text was corrupted. I cannot help but feel like I should have figured it out earlier. This is a lesson I have learned so many times, yet it keeps taking me by surprise even when I'm expecting it to be lurking around the corner.
